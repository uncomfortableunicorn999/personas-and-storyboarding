An in depth look at CVS self checkout

Description and Sketch

The CVS self checkout is a machine that consists of 4 main parts. There is the touch screen that has different screen based on where you are in the checkout process. The first screen is motion activated, when a user walks up to the checkout, it appears. The second. Sketches of the different screens are shown below. There is the scanner and scanning area that checks the items. Lastly there is the credit card pad/reader where the user swipes/taps/ApplePays and enters a pin on a keypad if necessary. There is also the bagging area which is important. The system keeps track of the weight in the bagging area and will get mad at the user if the weight does not increase after they scan an item.

Questions and Summary

Questions
  1. How did your interface experience make you feel?
  2. Why did you choose self checkout over normal checkout? and would you choose it again?
  3. Did you experience any challenges with the interface?
  4. Was the interface easy to use?
Observations
  • The majority of people had no issues using the system besides forgetting to click buttons or troubles scanning items
  • Everyone using self checkout had very few items
  • The bagging area is on the left on some machines despite the images on the screen instructing people to move their items to the right.
  • Not moving an item to the bagging area will cause an error
  • There were 3 main difficulties experienced: People not reading the instructions, the scanner not working, and the bagging area being unclear
  • Despite the difficulties all users said the system was easy to use
Summary
  • Most users either didn't have any reaction of a feeling from the interface. The ones that did felt confused, frustrated, or annoyed. The cause of these reaction were from something in the interface not functioning as it should
  • Some users chose self checkout because they believed it was faster, and they were in a rush, while others chose it because they don't like talking to people. A few of the interviewees chose it because the line was just shorter and it made more sense. Everyone said they would use it again despite any difficulties.
  • There were 3 main difficulties experienced: People not reading the instructions, the scanner not working, and the bagging area being unclear
  • Despite the difficulties all users said the system was easy to use

Personas

Awkward Alice is a woman in her twenties who gets very nervous in social interactions. She has troubles scanning her items and doesn't know where to put them when she has finished. Not placing them in the bagging area causes issues. She represents the class of users who choose might not know how to use self checkout, but choose because they don't want to talk to anyone
Impatient Ian is a busy man who is always in a hurry. Trying to go as fast as he can, Ian sometimes misses the all of the buttons on the touch screen that say "Continue" and then has to wait to realize what has happened. He represents the class of users who know how to use self checkout, and choose to do so because he believes it is faster.
